Biggest Opportunity

The Signup → Activation step loses 59% of users. Improving 7-day onboarding and time-to-first-value would have the highest leverage on downstream conversion and retention.

Funnel by Acquisition Channel
Activation %, paid conversion %, and 90-day retention % by channel
Key Finding

Referral leads on both activation (45.2%) and paid conversion (49.5%). It also has the highest avg LTV, making it the most efficient acquisition channel overall.

Channel Volume vs Conversion
User volume and paid conversion rate per channel
Key Finding

Organic Search drives the most signups (1,459) but converts at 45.2% — slightly below Referral. Email Campaign has lowest volume and lowest conversion (43.8%).

Churn Rate by Plan
Lifetime churn across the 18-month observation window
Key Finding

Enterprise plan churns at 43.7% vs 96.3% for free users — a 2.2× difference. Every plan upgrade significantly reduces churn risk. Growth → Enterprise upsells should be a retention priority.

Feature Engagement vs Churn
Users who adopt power features churn significantly less
Key Finding

Users who adopted 2+ power features (API, Automations, Integrations, Team Invite) churned at 68.7% vs 99.7% for low-engagement users. Power feature adoption is the single strongest predictor of retention.

Churn Rate by Acquisition Channel
Channel-level churn variation
Key Finding

Email Campaign has the highest churn (87.2%) despite lower volume — likely due to lower purchase intent vs organic channels. Paid Search & Referral produce the most retained paid users.

Retention Rate by Plan
Inverse of churn — % of users still active
Retention Focus Areas

Prioritize free-to-starter conversion (reduces churn 9pp) and power feature activation for starter users (reduces churn 30pp). These two levers drive the most retention improvement.
